Polar Bear

The polar bear is a hypercarnivorous species of bear.

Its native range lies largely within the Arctic Circle, encompassing the Arctic Ocean and its surrounding seas and landmasses, which includes the northernmost regions of North America and Eurasia

Height: 1.8 2.4 m (Female, Adult, On hind legs), 1.3 m (Male, Adult, At Shoulder)

Most polar bears occur north of the Arctic Circle to the North Pole.
There are some populations south of the Arctic Circle in the Hudson Bay of Manitoba, Canada.
Polar bears live in Alaska, Canada, Russia, Greenland, and some northern islands owned by Norway, such as Svalbard.
